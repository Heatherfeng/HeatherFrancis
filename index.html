What is Compassion?

Compassion is a profound, empathetic response to seeing others in distress. It drives us to help alleviate their suffering. This emotion is more intense and engaging than mere sympathy. It`s a fundamental part of being human, deeply rooted in our nature. Compassion arises from our ability to empathize and connect with others, a trait essential for building and maintaining social bonds. The need for compassion is universal. It plays a vital role in social interactions and personal well-being.
